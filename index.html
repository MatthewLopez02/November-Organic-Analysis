Key Finding

68.48% of November organic users (11,601 out of 16,939) have not completed any transactions since account creation. However, 5,338 users (31.52%) have activated their accounts, with 90.95% of those activating within 7 days of account creation.

Key Insight

Week 3 and Week 4 saw massive surges in organic acquisitions. Week 3 (Nov 15-21) brought 5,633 users, while Week 4 (Nov 22-30) was even larger with 8,343 users - representing 49.3% of the entire month's acquisitions. November 19th remained the single peak day at 1,978 users, but Nov 24-26 each saw 1,700+ users.

Daily Acquisition Trends

Daily Pattern Analysis

Organic acquisition spiked dramatically starting November 17th. The period from Nov 17-26 averaged 1,150 users/day, compared to just 213 users/day during Nov 1-16. This represents a 5.4x increase. Notable peaks: Nov 19 (1,978), Nov 24 (1,724), Nov 25 (1,720), and Nov 26 (1,715). Thanksgiving weekend (Nov 27-30) saw a decline to an average of 337 users/day.

Geographic Distribution

Key Insight

New York dominates organic acquisition with 26.08% of all users (4,418), with Brooklyn alone accounting for 2,096 users (12.4% of total). The top 10 states represent 68.3% of all organic users, indicating strong geographic concentration in the Northeast and major metro areas.

Feature Adoption Insight

Visa Money Transfer dominates with 25,877 transactions across 4,011 users (75.1% of active users). This indicates strong remittance use case adoption among organic users. Card Purchase is second with 19,311 transactions, showing healthy everyday spending usage. The high 7-day activation rate (90.95%) suggests that users who will transact do so quickly after account creation.

Activation Analysis

Activation Overview

68.48% of November organic users have not completed any transactions yet. While this represents 11,601 inactive users, the 31.52% activation rate shows strong engagement, and these active users are showing strong transaction engagement with an average of 12.5 transactions per active user.

Key Recommendations

Strategic Actions

  • Investigate Nov 17-26 Spike: The 5.4x increase in organic acquisition during this period warrants investigation to understand what drove this growth and how to sustain it.
  • Target Week 4 Cohort: With 75.51% still inactive (6,300 users), these users are still in the critical activation window. Deploy re-engagement campaigns immediately.
  • Remittance-First Onboarding: Since Visa Money Transfer is the #1 transaction type (75.1% of active users), emphasize remittance features in the onboarding flow.
  • 7-Day Window Focus: 90.95% of users who activate do so within 7 days. Concentrate all re-engagement efforts within this window.
  • Re-engage 11,601 Inactive Users: Deploy targeted push notifications and email campaigns to the inactive cohort with incentives for first transaction.
  • New York Focus: With 26% of organic users from New York (especially Brooklyn with 2,096 users), consider geo-targeted campaigns and local partnerships in the NYC metro area.
  • Capitalize on Volume: The consistent ~36-39% activation rate in Weeks 1-3 suggests acquisition quality is stable. Focus on increasing volume while maintaining this rate.
